Why The Proposed Model is Better

The Baseline Model relies on Exact Keyword Matching and basic TF-IDF term frequencies. It struggles with synonyms, misspellings, and semantically similar ingredients (e.g., treating "sweet potato" and "potato" completely differently or failing on "tomto" instead of "tomato").

The Proposed Model utilizes exactly what modern architecture expects: Semantic Embeddings via SentenceTransformers and Fuzzy NLP Matching via SpaCy. This means it inherently understands that "chicken breast" is a type of "chicken" and is much more forgiving with real-world, noisy OCR data. Furthermore, its rebalanced scoring weights emphasize Coverage (50%) over raw Semantic text similarity (30%), optimizing for practical cookability.
